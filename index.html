Subway Stations Accessibility Infrastructure

What the MTA is doing

The project is dedicated to enhancing accessibility across the transit system, with a focus on initiatives outlined in the MTA's 2020-2024 Capital Program. This program allocates a historic $5.2 billion towards making stations newly accessible, alongside an additional nearly $600 million designated for elevator upgrades to enhance reliability.

Utilizing these funds, the project aims to render certain subway stations newly accessible while implementing elevator replacements and other enhancements to ensure reliability and safety. Upon completion, subway riders will find accessible stations within a maximum of two stops' distance.

Progress has already commenced, with accessibility upgrades underway at 21 stations across the five boroughs, and elevator replacement projects recently finalized at eight stations, with ongoing efforts throughout the system. The Rapid Station Accessibility Upgrade program is facilitating some of these improvements through collaboration with a private partner tasked with expedited and cost-effective execution, alongside a 15-year maintenance commitment for the elevators.

Furthermore, the 57 St F station is slated for accessibility enhancements under the Zoning for Accessibility program, established through a partnership with New York City and private developers.

Moreover, a landmark agreement announced in 2022 between the MTA and accessibility advocates solidifies the commitment to systemwide accessibility, outlining a clear trajectory towards achieving 95% accessibility by 2055. Strategic decisions regarding station prioritization for accessibility improvements are made in accordance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), and through consultation with riders and advocates to maximize the project's impact.
