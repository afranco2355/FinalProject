Subway Stations Accessibility Infrastructure

What the MTA is doing

Check Elevator and Escalator Status

The MTA is dedicated to enhancing accessibility across the subway system, with a focus on initiatives outlined in the MTA's 2020-2024 Capital Program. This program allocates a historic $5.2 billion towards making stations newly accessible, alongside an additional nearly $600 million designated for elevator upgrades to enhance reliability.

Utilizing these funds, the project aims to render certain subway stations newly accessible while implementing elevator replacements and other enhancements to ensure reliability and safety. Upon completion, subway riders will find accessible stations within a maximum of two stops' distance.

Progress has already commenced, with accessibility upgrades underway at 21 stations across the five boroughs, and elevator replacement projects recently finalized at eight stations, with ongoing efforts throughout the system. However, the MTA still has a lot of work to do since just 26% of stations are accessible and much of the existing infrastructure is not well-maintained, leading to potential out-of-service instances that affect people with disabilities, families with babies, and any other user in need of accessible infrastructure like elevators and escalators. This map illustrates that, despite the MTA's efforts, they are still behind, and there is much work to be done to guarantee accessibility and equity in the subway system.
